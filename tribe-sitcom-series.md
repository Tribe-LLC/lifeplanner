# Tribe Sitcom Series — "Planner Problems"

A recurring content series inspired by sitcom energy. Every episode captures a relatable, funny moment where planning (or the lack of it) meets real life. Think "The Office" meets your to-do list.

## Series Concept

**Series Name**: Planner Problems

- **The Funny One** — comedy, unexpected twists, meme energy

## Episode 4 — "The 3 AM To-Do List"
**Mood**: The Serious One

### Instagram Reel / TikTok

**Script**:

HOOK (0-3s):
*Dark room. Phone screen lights up showing 3:17 AM.*
*Text overlay*: "When your brain decides 3 AM is the perfect time for a to-do list"

BUILDUP (3-15s):
*Person lying in bed, eyes wide open*
*Thought bubbles appearing as text overlays (rapid fire)*:
- "Did I pay the electricity bill"
- "I need to text mom back"
- "That thing I said in 2019 was so awkward"
- "What's the plan for the presentation"
- "Do we have milk"
- "Am I saving enough for retirement"
- "I should learn a language"
- "What is my purpose"

*Each one appears faster, overlapping*

THE TURN (15-22s):
*Person sighs. Reaches for their planner on the nightstand.*
*Turns on a soft lamp. Opens to a blank page.*
*Starts writing — slowly, calmly.*
*Text overlay*: "Brain dump. Get it out of your head."

*The thought bubbles literally fade from the screen as they write*

RESOLUTION (22-30s):
*Person closes planner. Takes a breath. Puts it back on nightstand.*
*Lies back down. Eyes close.*
*Text overlay*: "It's still there in the morning. But now it's on paper, not on repeat."

**Caption (Instagram)**:
The 3 AM brain spiral is the worst kind of productivity.

Your brain isn't trying to help you plan at 3 AM. It's trying to process everything you didn't get to during the day.

The fix isn't forcing yourself to sleep. It's giving those thoughts somewhere to go.

Keep a planner or notebook by your bed. When the spiral starts, do a brain dump. Write everything down — messy, unfiltered, no categories.

You're not solving anything at 3 AM. You're just moving it from your head to paper so your brain can let go.

Try it tonight. You'll be surprised how quickly it works.

### Facebook

Can we normalize keeping a planner on your nightstand?

Not for morning journaling (though that's great too). For the 3 AM brain spiral.

You know the one. You're sleeping fine, and then suddenly your brain decides NOW is the time to remember every undone task, unsent email, and questionable life decision from the past decade.

The thing is, you can't think your way out of it. Your brain is in processing mode, not problem-solving mode. Trying to "figure things out" at 3 AM just feeds the spiral.

What actually works: a brain dump. Grab your planner, open to any blank page, and write everything your brain is throwing at you. No order. No categories. Just get it out.

Something almost magical happens when those thoughts move from your head to paper. Your brain recognizes they're captured. They're not going to be forgotten. And it lets go.

The tasks are still there in the morning. But now they're on a page with a plan instead of on a loop in your head.
